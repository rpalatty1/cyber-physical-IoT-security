B4: The most common sizes in UID access cards are 4-byte (single UID), 7-byte (double UID) and 10-byte (triple UID). It isn’t one size as the UIDs will vary based on the card type and properties. Eg, the 4-byte is quite common and found in older mifare classic cards, whereas the 10-byte is less common and used for high security as it has a lower collision rate due to its UIDs containing more values.

Stock cards from the manufacturer are usually more expensive and personalised, with unique non-default encrypted keys, which makes it more secure than ‘magic cards’.
